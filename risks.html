Risks

Main Threats

Just as fast as these developments are being progressed, so are the threats of browsing. This resource that was once used to solely share information, is now evolving far beyond that. With that evolution, threats begin to make their appearance in nearly every form and fashion. While browsing, users must stay vigilant for any kind of attack as browsers are one of the most popular forms of attack. As America’s Cyber Defense Agency details, “Web browsers are very attractive targets for cyber threat actors since they are one of the most commonly used apps” (America’s Cyber Defense Agency). The first thing a user does after entering a prompt into a browser is select and click a link. These links to websites or extensions can be just about anything. The link could be exactly what the user is looking for, or that one click could give away several personal details without you realizing it even happened. Browsers are often the gateway to anything that is done on the internet and thus are used to make people vulnerable. Once simple click can have your information stolen, a virus downloaded, or simply just lead to misinformation. Most browsers have some form of detection to help protect you from these things. Going even further, whatever device that is being used to access the browser also usually has layers of defense to help protect you against these threats. Unfortunately, no matter how good a defender may be, an inexperienced user can still be prone to giving up information that could ruin their life. Due to this, entire companies are created to develop countermeasures to these schemes. Software Analyst Cyber Research says that, “This has led to the rise of newer technologies that are focused on securing the browser against threats such as malware, malicious redirects, and phishing, regardless of the device or location” (Software Analyst Cyber Research). Without these advancements in cybersecurity, the browser experience would be one that no one would dare touch for fear of losing far more than one could ever gain. Staying up to date with how browser security and threats work are both key in navigating the online space safely and without worry.
