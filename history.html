History

Past

Similar to all technologies, browsing started in an infant stage that could barely be recognized in today's standards as “browsing.” The real beginning can be traced back to the idea of what browsing may become.

As stated in History of the Web, “An influential early exploration of how computers might revolutionize information is a 1945 essay by Vannevar Bush entitled ‘As We May Think’. This essay envisioned a machine called a memex that helps an individual human see and explore all the information in the world” (History of the Web).

This shows how the concept of the “web” or even browsing was thought of before it could be created. Development for this did not actually begin until around 1988, when the first iteration of an online database was created.

The History of the Web mentions, “Users can download a WAIS (Wide Area Information Servers) client to search through an array of distributed servers and access the documents stored there” (The History of the Web). With the creation of WAIS that can be shared and searched, one of the first ideas of browsing came to life.

After this idea is then passed from hand to hand and developed over the years, the first browser comes to life. This machine was the WorldWideWeb browser. Though it was advanced for its time, it did not gain much traction initially.

Tim Berners-Lee publishes the first website as a way of demonstrating what the web was and explaining its purpose. This step sped up development and helped browsing take off.

Developments such as HTTP, URLs, and Lynx followed, expanding what browsing could accomplish. At this point, browsing was still basic, but the foundation was set.

Present

The modern day usage and applications of browsing have changed just as much as the development of browsing. The original goal was to simply create and share information on a large scale.

As Surfing the Web explains, “Web browsers serve as our virtual passports to the internet, enabling us to access websites and explore the vast expanse of online content.”

This gateway introduces users to a nearly infinite source of information. However, modern browsing also includes misinformation, clickbait, and unreliable sources alongside valuable content.

Websites now include games, blogs, AI tools, and interactive services. Browsing has evolved from static documents into fully interactive experiences.

Future developments include mobile browsing, virtual reality integration, and voice-controlled browsing. The technology is changing rapidly and continues to evolve.

As Surfing the Web states, “Whether you are a casual internet user or a tech enthusiast, understanding the intricacies of web browsers and the internet can empower you to make the most of this incredible tool.”

This shows how quickly the present becomes the past in the digital world.
